About Me

A bit more about who I am outside of school

Fishing

I grew up in Minnesota, so fishing has always been a big part of my life. One of my best memories is catching a huge northern pike on Lake Superior late in the summer. The fog was coming in, the water was completely flat, and I fought that fish for a good 20 minutes before I got it to the boat. That feeling is why I keep going back.

I'll fish just about anywhere. Fly fishing on the Lester River, trolling for lake trout, ice fishing on a frozen bay. It's taught me a lot about patience and just slowing down, which honestly helps with everything else too.

Snowboarding

Spirit Mountain is right here in Duluth, so it was pretty much impossible not to get into snowboarding. I still remember my first real run down the black diamond. My legs were shaking the whole way, but by the time I hit the bottom I was hooked.

I try to ride at least a couple times a week during the season. Night riding after a long day of classes is hard to beat. Cold air in your face, fresh groomed snow, and nobody else out there. It's the best way to clear your head.

Hiking

Duluth has some of the best trails in the state right outside your door. I spend a lot of time on the Superior Hiking Trail, sometimes just a quick loop after class, sometimes a full day up along the ridgeline above the lake. The views never get old.

Lester Park is probably where I go the most. The river cuts through this gorge, and after a good rain the waterfalls are awesome. It's a solid reset when school gets stressful. Honestly, hiking is a big reason I picked Duluth in the first place.
